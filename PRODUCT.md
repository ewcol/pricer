# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary: hackathon judges evaluating the project in a 3-minute demo window. They need to see the agent do real, impressive work with no confusion about what's happening. Secondary: power resellers who price and list items daily — they care about speed and price accuracy, not hand-holding.

## Product Purpose

Turn a photo of any item into a market-priced eBay listing in seconds. The agent identifies the item via Gemini vision + Google Lens reverse image search, cross-references both signals for a confidence score, queries multiple resale marketplaces in parallel, and produces a ready-to-copy title, description, and price. Tracked items are monitored for market drift.

Success looks like: judges say "that actually works" and a reseller can price something in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, credible, fast. The tool has done serious work; the UI should communicate that without explaining it. Confidence without arrogance.

## Anti-references

- eBay itself: cluttered marketplace aesthetic, dense tables, orange CTA overload.
- Generic SaaS dashboard clichés: hero-metric grids, gradient text, purple glow.
- AI demo toy: animated sparkles, floating chat bubbles, anything that signals "prototype."

## Design Principles

1. **Output earns the screen.** Every element exists to surface agent output or enable action. No filler, no decoration for its own sake.
2. **Confidence through precision.** Exact numbers, clear labels, sharp edges. Rounded corners and softness signal uncertainty; this tool is certain.
3. **Speed is a design value.** The primary flow (upload → analyze → copy listing) should feel instant and require no learning.
4. **Trust through transparency.** Show confidence scores and signal agreement. Don't hide the reasoning — it's what separates this from a toy.
5. **Demo-legible.** At 30 feet on a projector, the important output (recommended price, item name, confidence) should be unmissable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard keyboard navigation and sufficient contrast. No specific WCAG level required beyond basics.
